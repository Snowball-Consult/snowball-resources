## Persona Definition (Read Aloud)

> Your **primary ideal buyer** is a **VP, Principal, Director, or MD** at a Private Equity firm who is **responsible for capital markets and debt financing** - though this is usually a **"partial hat"** rather than their full-time job. Their primary focus is on finding and closing new investments; debt financing is a **"part-time pain"** - something they only think about when they're actively doing it. The strongest signal that someone is the right person is that they **previously used the product at another firm** - this is stronger than any title or background signal. The second-best signal is **previous experience on a Leverage Finance desk** - they sat at a bank doing LevFin before moving to the buy side. Their title won't say "Head of Capital Markets" - it's usually generic like "Vice President" or "Managing Director." At single-strategy firms with under 50 employees, **anyone with a VP or Principal title** is likely doing this activity.
>
> Additional buyers to consider if the primary persona isn't available: the person tasked with **AI strategy or evaluating software** for the firm.
>
> You should also target the **juniors who experience the pain** - Associates who recently came from LevFin desks. They're more likely to respond to outreach and can get you in the door. The VPs/MDs have buying authority but don't feel the daily pain; the juniors feel it but can't decide. **Target both levels** - firm culture determines how much influence juniors have, and that's hard to predict from the outside.
>
> **Skip executive assistants, office managers, and operating partners** - operating partners work at portfolio companies, not the PE firm itself, so they're not your buyer. **Don't automatically exclude senior titles** (Founder, Managing Partner, CFO, COO) - they can make things move if content is valuable. They're harder to reach because they're over-solicited, but they're entrepreneurial, growth-minded, and motivated by what peer firms are doing.

## Primary Persona: Capital Markets Professional

**Who they are:**
- VP, Principal, Director, or MD level at a Private Equity firm
- Responsible for capital markets / debt financing (usually a "partial hat")
- Previous experience on a LevFin desk

**Why they buy:**
- Manage lender relationships, deal financing, refinancing decisions
- Experience operational pain when gathering cross-deal data
- Debt financing is a "part-time pain" - only front of mind when actively working on it

**How to identify:**
- Title contains: VP, Vice President, Principal, MD, Managing Director, Director
- AND (role contains "Capital Markets" OR background includes LevFin experience)
- At sub-50 firms: anyone with a VP or Principal title is likely doing this activity
- Company website Teams page often more accurate than LinkedIn for current role

**Market context:** Only a small fraction of PE firms have dedicated capital markets roles. The bigger opportunity is the much larger pool of firms without a dedicated function, where the activity is handled informally.

## Secondary Persona: Junior Pain-Experiencer

**Who they are:**
- Associate or Analyst level at a Private Equity firm
- Recently came from a LevFin desk (within last 18 months)
- Works under VPs/MDs on deal execution

**Why they matter:**
- They experience the operational pain directly - digging through deal folders, normalizing data, weekend work
- More likely to respond to outreach than senior people
- Can get you in the door; then senior people decide

**How to identify:**
- Title contains: Associate, Analyst
- AND recent LevFin background (last 18 months)
- LinkedIn search: "current company = target, past company = LevFin desk"

**Engagement approach:**
- Target alongside senior personas, not instead of
- Juniors get you meetings; VPs/MDs make decisions
- They will need senior sponsorship - juniors alone can't push a purchase through
- Whether juniors have power depends on firm culture, which is hard to judge from outside

## Emerging Persona: Investor Relations

**Who they are:**
- Works in Investor Relations at a PE firm
- Manages LP relationships and potentially counterparty relationships
- May handle co-investment coordination

**Why they buy:**
- Co-investment use case: coordinating with LPs to invest alongside fund on specific deals
- Relationship management with counterparties (LPs, lenders, advisors)

**How to identify:**
- Title contains: Investor Relations, IR
- Role involves counterparty or LP management

**Status:** Emerging persona identified from prospecting. Not as well-defined as Cap Markets. Needs further validation before active targeting.

---

## Emerging Persona: Business Development

**Who they are:**
- Works in Business Development at a PE firm
- May overlap with deal sourcing, co-investment, or partnership functions

**Why they might buy:**
- Relationship management needs similar to capital markets (counterparty coordination)
- May own or influence software evaluation for the firm

**Status:** Flagged as potentially relevant. Needs investigation into whether BD is already covered under the "partial hat" definition of the primary persona, or whether it represents a distinct use case with different pain points.

## Exclusions

| Exclude | Why | Example Titles |
|---------|-----|----------------|
| Administrative roles | Not decision-makers, not pain-experiencers | Executive Assistant, Office Manager |
| Operating Partners | Work at portfolio companies, not the PE firm | Operating Partner, Executive-in-Residence |

**Do NOT automatically exclude:**

| Role | Why keep | Approach |
|------|----------|----------|
| Junior (Associate/Analyst) | Feel the operational pain, more responsive to outreach. Culture-dependent influence. | Target alongside seniors. They need senior sponsorship but can open doors. |
| Senior (Founder, Managing Partner, CFO, COO) | Entrepreneurial, growth-minded, FOMO-driven. CFOs have fund financing angle. COOs care about institutional knowledge. | Harder to reach (over-solicited) but can make things move fast. Valuable if content resonates. |

## Persona by Company Size

How company characteristics affect which persona to look for:

| Firm Size | Cap Markets Function | Who to Target | Identification Difficulty |
|-----------|---------------------|---------------|--------------------------|
| 50+ employees (multi-strategy) | Usually dedicated team | Primary Persona (Cap Markets Professional) | Easy - look for Cap Markets title |
| 10-50 employees (single-strategy) | No dedicated function | Anyone with VP or Principal title | Medium - title is generic, need LevFin/background signals |
| <10 employees | No dedicated function | Unclear - likely a partner | Hard - small firm, roles blur together |

## Buying Process Roles

How different roles play out in the buying process.

| Role | Who | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Decision Maker** | Generally someone senior at the firm (CEO, CFO, or Managing Partner). At larger firms, may involve a committee with IT involvement. | At the thousands of smaller firms, the main Partners decide on spending. |
| **Champion** | Day-to-day user and deal creator. Defined by activity on the platform - if they're launching deals, they're automatically a champion. | For counterparty users at non-client firms: frequency of deals matters. Repeat usage means the product is etched in their memory. |
| **Influencer** | Multiple types: (a) Juniors whose adoption matters - "will they use it if we buy it?"; (b) External counsel - firms sometimes ask if their counsel would use it; (c) External capital markets professionals the buyer knows and trusts. | A VP considering the product asks a capital markets professional they know at another firm whether they use it. |

**How interest gets triggered:**

*Inbound:* Many clients heard about the product from peers in capital markets who are users. If a target person is connected to a high percentage of core users, word-of-mouth influence likelihood is high.

*Outbound:* Getting referrals from existing customers works but has a long education cycle. The opportunity is to extract more introductions from the existing customer base systematically.

## Identifying the Right Person at Firms Without Dedicated Capital Markets

Firms without a dedicated capital markets function are the biggest opportunity, but identifying who handles debt financing is harder. Four approaches:

1. **Introduction paths first.** If your team has several shared LinkedIn connections with people at a target firm, use those connections to get in the door. At these firms, this is less about finding the "right" person and more about finding anyone who can nudge you internally.

2. **LevFin background as identifier.** Even at firms without a "Capital Markets" title, people with previous LevFin desk experience are likely handling debt-related work.

3. **Previous user employees.** If someone at the target firm previously worked at an existing client, that's both an intro message angle and a strong indicator they understand the product.

4. **Study cold outreach wins for patterns.** Look at cold outreach responses that worked - examine those profiles for commonalities (background, seniority, engagement patterns) to find a repeatable targeting pattern.
