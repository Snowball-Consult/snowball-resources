What IntroPath Is

IntroPath is Capstone's term for their warm introduction process - finding the best path into a target account through people who already know and trust you. In enterprise sales, cold outreach barely works. Warm intros are the GTM motion that actually converts.

"I ran this five minutes on Monday, got a new account on Tuesday, and this is what works. Everything else is just cool - we are doing it for the sake of doing it, but this works."
Arjun Mehta, on the IntroPath motion

How It Works Today

1
Identify target company
Open LinkedIn Sales Navigator, view all contacts at a target firm
2
Sort by mutual connections
Find who at the target firm has the most overlap with your network
3
Cross-reference approved referrer list
Check the mutual connections against a manually maintained Excel sheet of ~20-50 trusted referrers
4
Manually assess relationship strength
Guess whether the referrer actually knows the target well enough for a real introduction
5
Ask for the introduction
Draft an email to the referrer asking them to connect you with the target
6
Often hear: "I don't really know them well"
Days wasted. The LinkedIn connection existed, but the actual relationship didn't.
The Bottleneck
The sales team doesn't have deep industry networks. They default to asking Arjun for every warm introduction - and Arjun becomes the bottleneck. The approved referrer list lives in an Excel sheet that isn't maintained. IntroPath works when Arjun runs it personally, but it doesn't scale beyond him.

What It Needs to Become

1
Reduce false intro requests
Technology should identify which paths have actual relationship strength - not just LinkedIn connections. A connection from 10 years ago with no interaction is meaningless. Someone with frequent, recent communication is a real relationship.
2
Stack-rank contacts by propensity
Instead of manually picking 3-4 people to ask, automatically rank all contacts at a target company by likelihood of knowing the target - same school, post interactions, shared meetings, actual communication patterns.
3
Programmatic exclusion lists
Automatically filter out companies already in the sales process, lenders when you're targeting sponsors, and contacts outside the target list. Today these filters live in Arjun's head.
THE IDEAL FLOW
1 Sales rep sees a target contact in HubSpot
2 System returns ranked introduction paths with relationship strength scores
3 Rep sees: "Ask Sarah (strong) to introduce you to Bob - they went to Wharton together and exchange emails monthly"
4 Rep asks the right person. Intro lands. No days wasted.

What Graph One Does

Graph One is a relationship intelligence platform that maps professional networks and identifies introduction paths. The platform uses deep learning to evaluate actual relationship strength from communication patterns - not just connection counts.

Relationship Strength Scoring
Deep learning model evaluates LinkedIn connections, DM exchanges, post interactions, email frequency, calendar meetings, and communication recency. Distinguishes strong relationships from stale connections.
Multi-Hop Path Finding
Returns ranked introduction paths: "You know Alice (strong). Alice knows Bob (medium). This is your best path to Bob." Multiple paths per target, ranked by overall strength.
API-First Architecture
Everything accessible via API. Can integrate with HubSpot, Clay, or any system. Build the experience that fits the workflow - no dependency on Graph One's UI.
Enterprise On-Prem Deployment
For data-sensitive environments: deploy entirely within the client's own cloud. Data never leaves the client's environment. Only sync state is shared with the network.
Team Onboarding
Google Workspace admin can batch-authenticate all team accounts at once. Each team member does a ~30-minute calibration session to review and refine their network quality. More people connected means better path coverage across the organization.
